Awarded the "Data Award"

Upon discovering this competitive hackathon, I parted from my school's marching band during a football game and drove nearly four hours to Cleveland, OH so that I could develop my own research project and compete.

I captained my research team, working alongside my sister. We spent seven hours at the NASA Glenn Research Center researching the topic of algal blooms, and were ready to present and compete by the end of the day.

Although this trip was spontaneous, and the final project was completed in a total of seven hours, I am fortunate to have had an opportuntiy to research a topic I was invested in and present my knowledge and work to a panel of NASA scientists and employees.

Finalist

During the pandemic, when we were all trapped at home and searching for new hobbies to try out, I came across the PoSSUM (Polar Suborbital Science in the Upper Mesosphere) 13 Microgravity Challenge.

I loved the idea of the competition, because I could take my creativity into a scientific realm. I partnered up with my best friend and got to work right away. I was chosen as a finalist for the competition, and was able to work with an incredible team of female scientists, who further encouraged my dream of becoming a woman in STEM.

Pursuing my love for space science is something I passionately aspire to do everyday, and I was thrilled to do so in this internship.

A majority of my summer was spent learning about Earth and space science that I wouldn't typically study in school, and also doing scientific research of my own with a team of incredible students who quickly became my friends.

This was a life changing experience, where I was able to pursue my dream of doing scientific research alongside NASA, and also making lifetime friends in the process.
